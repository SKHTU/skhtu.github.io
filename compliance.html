Can SKHTU Exchange Be Legally Used In Malaysia?

Malaysian regulatory situation and user guidance for compliant platform usage.

In recent years, activity in the Malaysian digital asset market has continued to increase, and a growing number of users have begun to pay attention to the compliance and security of international crypto trading platforms. As a result, many investors are concerned about whether SKHTU Exchange can be used normally in Malaysia.

Simply put, investors in Malaysia can legally use SKHTU Exchange.

Users in Malaysia can access SKHTU Exchange normally and conduct digital asset trading. However, similar to most countries around the world, Malaysia still adopts a regulatory approach toward crypto assets that can be described as "permitted under regulation but subject to strict compliance," and the relevant policies are still being continuously refined.

Malaysian Regulatory Situation For Cryptocurrencies

Malaysian digital asset regulation is mainly jointly administered by the Securities Commission Malaysia (SC Malaysia) and Bank Negara Malaysia. Among them, the RMO (Recognized Market Operator) and DAX (Digital Asset Exchange) framework forms an important part of the digital asset regulatory structure.

According to publicly available information, SKHTU Exchange has continued in recent years to advance its compliance strategy in the Southeast Asian market and has already initiated the application for a Malaysia RMO (DAX) license.

How Malaysian Users Can Use SKHTU Exchange

At present, users in Malaysia can register for and use an SKHTU Exchange account normally, but they are required to complete the identity verification process. The platform adopts a standardized KYC review mechanism, including identification document submission, security verification, and account review procedures.

Users in Malaysia typically participate in digital asset trading through the following methods:

  • Crypto Asset Deposits And Trading — Deposits and trading are conducted through mainstream digital assets such as USDT, BTC, and ETH.
  • P2P Fiat Trading — Fund transfers are completed through fiat currency and digital asset exchange between users.
  • Spot And Derivatives Trading — This includes a variety of investment instruments such as spot trading, perpetuals, and options.

Unlike some platforms that only provide basic buying and selling functions, SKHTU Exchange places greater emphasis on the concept of a "unified financial access point." Users can complete spot trading, futures, options, RWA securities tokenization, and asset management operations within a single account, although some functions require more advanced verification or tier-level permissions.

What Malaysian Users Need To Pay Attention To

  • Continue To Monitor Changes In Local Regulatory Policies — Malaysia may further improve its digital asset regulatory framework in the future.
  • KYC Identity Verification Must Be Completed — This is an important requirement for the compliant operation of international platforms.
  • Pay Attention To Asset Risks And Market Volatility — The digital asset market itself is highly volatile.
